1. PURPOSE

Define Failure Injection Tests (FIT) for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Validates system robustness and resilience under controlled fault conditions
Ensures reactive control, safety, and watchdog mechanisms respond predictably
Supports auditability and reproducibility of fault-handling behavior
2. TEST OBJECTIVES
Simulate hardware and software faults to validate safety systems
Verify watchdog and kill-line respond correctly
Test authority escalation and reactive safety mechanisms
Confirm system logs all events and maintains operational integrity
Evaluate subsystem fault tolerance and recovery procedures
3. TEST SETUP
Environment: lab-controlled test bench or isolated outdoor area
Deployment Profile: FIELD_STANDARD or ENERGY_SAVER as required
Monitoring: ROS2 topics, actuator telemetry, energy system, watchdog logs
Safety Precautions: emergency stop ready, redundant kill-line engaged
4. FAILURE INJECTION PROCEDURE

Step 1 – Initialization:

Power up system and confirm all subsystems nominal
Activate logging and telemetry capture
Verify watchdog and kill-line operational

Step 2 – Inject Software Faults:

ROS2 node crash: terminate a mission-critical node and observe recovery
Message drop: block select ROS2 topics to simulate network loss
Authority override: attempt invalid remote command and verify rejection

Step 3 – Inject Hardware Faults:

Battery undervoltage: simulate low SOC and verify energy-aware halt
Actuator stall or overcurrent: trigger via controlled load and monitor response
Sensor failure: disable or misreport IMU/LiDAR readings and verify safe behavior

Step 4 – Observe and Record:

Confirm watchdog triggers kill-line if critical fault occurs
Reactive controller enforces safe state
All subsystem telemetry, logs, and fault reports captured

Step 5 – Recovery Testing:

Reset failed nodes or restore hardware function
Validate smooth resumption of normal operations
Ensure no persistent unsafe states remain
5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
Watchdog triggers kill-line within 50 ms of critical fault
Reactive control maintains safe posture or halts motion
Authority model prevents unsafe remote command execution
System logs all fault events with timestamps and subsystem identification
Recovery occurs without residual errors or safety violations
6. FAILSAFE AND MONITORING
Redundant kill-line ensures immediate halt during critical failure
Safety overrides operate independently of ROS2 or main compute
Continuous monitoring of actuators, sensors, and energy system
All events automatically logged for post-test audit
7. DESIGN INTENT
Ensure predictable, safe, and auditable response to all simulated faults
Validate integration of mechanical, electrical, software, and control subsystems
Maintain fleet readiness and mission assurance
Provide repeatable failure injection tests for development and QA cycles
Enable continuous improvement of resilience and safety mechanisms
