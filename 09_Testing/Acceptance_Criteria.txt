1. PURPOSE

Define acceptance criteria for OBSIDIAN-8 V3 to ensure all systems meet functional, safety, and performance requirements before deployment.

Provides clear, measurable pass/fail conditions
Ensures reproducibility and auditability of tests
Validates integration of mechanical, electrical, software, and swarm subsystems
2. TEST CATEGORIES
Mechanical & Actuation: stability, load tolerance, gait accuracy
Electrical & Energy Systems: voltage stability, current draw, BMS integration
Software & Control: ROS2 node reliability, authority model, watchdog functionality
Perception & Navigation: sensor accuracy, SLAM mapping, terrain classification
Swarm & Network: communication integrity, swarm task execution, remote mission control
Safety & Failsafe: emergency stop, kill-line, hazard response
3. MECHANICAL ACCEPTANCE
Load Testing: structural frame withstands rated payload ±10%
Gait Accuracy: leg trajectory deviation ≤ 2% from commanded path
Tripod & Stair Mode: robot maintains balance on ±15° incline
Actuator Response: joint position error ≤ 0.5° under nominal load
4. ELECTRICAL ACCEPTANCE
Battery System: SOC measurement accuracy ±3%, cutoff triggers correctly
Power Distribution: rails maintain voltage within ±5% under load
BMS Integration: overcurrent, undervoltage, and thermal limits enforce shutdown
Watchdog: kill-line triggers within 50 ms of fault detection
5. SOFTWARE ACCEPTANCE
ROS2 Nodes: all required nodes launch without errors
Authority Model: local safety overrides remote commands in all test scenarios
Deployment Profiles: correct software, firmware, and configuration loaded per profile
Logging: all critical events recorded with timestamps, no missing entries
Duet/Maestro Interfaces: actuator commands executed accurately, feedback verified
6. PERCEPTION & NAVIGATION ACCEPTANCE
SLAM & Mapping: positional error ≤ 5 cm in indoor/outdoor test environments
Terrain Classification: ≥ 95% classification accuracy on benchmark surfaces
Obstacle Detection: ≥ 98% detection rate for obstacles ≥ 5 cm in height
Path Planning: generated paths avoid obstacles and maintain planned speed/trajectory
7. SWARM & NETWORK ACCEPTANCE
Heartbeat & Communication: >99% message delivery in multi-unit simulations
Task Assignment: all units execute tasks correctly within ±5% of assigned timing
Remote Mission Control: operator commands received and executed within latency bounds
Secure Communications: TLS and authentication verified; no unauthorized messages accepted
8. SAFETY ACCEPTANCE
Kill-Line & Watchdog: emergency stop triggered reliably in all failure simulations
Reactive Control Overrides: prevents unsafe motion under fault or miscommand
Energy-Aware Navigation: robot halts safely on low battery or hazardous terrain
Field Failures: robot maintains predictable, safe state during communication loss or sensor failure
9. ACCEPTANCE PROCESS
Pre-Test Setup: verify deployment profile, energy levels, and system readiness
Execution: perform mechanical, electrical, software, perception, swarm, and safety tests
Validation: record all measurements, logs, and video evidence
Pass/Fail Determination: compare results to acceptance thresholds in sections 3–8
Audit: all tests logged and stored for review and regulatory compliance
10. DESIGN INTENT
Ensure all OBSIDIAN-8 units meet mission-ready operational standards
Validate integration of mechanical, electrical, software, perception, and swarm systems
Provide objective, auditable, and reproducible criteria for deployment approval
Guarantee safety-first operation under all test conditions
Maintain a traceable record for post-deployment review and continuous improvement
