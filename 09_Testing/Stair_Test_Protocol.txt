1. PURPOSE

Define the Stair Test Protocol (STP) for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Validates robot stair-climbing mechanics, gait adaptation, and safety systems
Ensures integration of perception, control, energy management, and mechanical actuation
Provides measurable pass/fail criteria for deployment readiness
2. TEST OBJECTIVES
Verify safe and stable stair ascent and descent
Measure joint positions, step clearance, and lift accuracy
Validate actuator performance under dynamic load
Test reactive safety and authority escalation during missteps
Monitor energy consumption and system temperature
Capture logs for audit and post-test analysis
3. TEST SETUP
Environment: standard test staircase or modular stair simulator
Deployment Profile: FIELD_STANDARD or PERFORMANCE_MODE
Sensors & Monitoring: IMU, encoders, force sensors, ROS2 topics
Logging: joint telemetry, ROS2 command/feedback, energy consumption, actuator temperatures
4. TEST PROCEDURE

Step 1 – Initialization:

Power up system and verify subsystem readiness
Confirm deployment profile and baseline configuration
Activate logging and telemetry capture

Step 2 – Approach & Step Detection:

Execute approach trajectory to stairs
Use perception system to detect step height, depth, and orientation
Confirm reactive control can correct minor misalignments

Step 3 – Stair Ascent:

Command stair-climbing gait
Measure foot placement, step clearance, and joint angles
Monitor actuator torque and load for each step
Verify robot maintains balance and stability

Step 4 – Stair Descent:

Execute descent trajectory
Measure controlled lowering of body and foot placement
Ensure energy absorption and reactive control maintain stability
Record all actuator and energy data

Step 5 – Disturbance Simulation:

Apply lateral or backward perturbation during ascent/descent
Confirm reactive safety layer prevents tipping
Log recovery performance and timing

Step 6 – Continuous Loop Test:

Repeat ascent/descent cycles over multiple runs (≥5)
Monitor actuator temperature, energy consumption, and ROS2 node stability
Ensure no cumulative errors or safety violations
5. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
Step clearance ≥ nominal + 5 mm, no foot collisions
Joint torque within ±5% of safe operating limits
Stability maintained with ≤1° roll/pitch deviation per step
Recovery from perturbations within 1 s
Energy consumption and actuator temperatures within deployment profile limits
ROS2 nodes and logging remain operational; no kill-line activation
6. FAILSAFE AND MONITORING
Watchdog monitors system responsiveness and triggers kill-line if fault occurs
Reactive control prevents falls, overextension, or unsafe movement
Energy and thermal thresholds trigger safe slowdown or halt
Emergency stop remains available at all times
7. DESIGN INTENT
Ensure safe, reliable, and repeatable stair traversal under all test conditions
Validate integration of mechanical, control, perception, and energy systems
Provide measurable metrics for acceptance and fleet deployment
Confirm robustness of reactive safety and authority control layers
Maintain traceable records for post-test audit and mission readiness
