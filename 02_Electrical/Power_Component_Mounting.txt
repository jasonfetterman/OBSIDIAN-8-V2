1. PURPOSE

Define mounting, placement, and mechanical integration of power system components for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Ensures secure installation of batteries, BMS, regulators, and distribution hardware
Prevents vibration damage, thermal issues, and electrical faults
Supports maintenance access and modular replacement
Aligns with Frame Architecture and Cable Routing standards
2. COMPONENT OVERVIEW

Power-related components include:

Battery modules (LiFePO4 / LiPo)
Battery Management System (BMS)
Power Distribution Board (PDB)
DC-DC regulators
Main disconnect / fuse assemblies

All components must be mounted within designated chassis zones.

3. MOUNTING LOCATIONS
Component	Location	Notes
Battery Modules	Central lower chassis	Low CG priority
BMS	Adjacent to battery	Minimize wiring length
Power Distribution Board	Central backbone panel	Equal access to all subsystems
DC-DC Regulators	Ventilated side panels	Heat dissipation required
Main Fuse / Disconnect	Accessible exterior panel	Emergency access
Layout must preserve center of gravity alignment per Mass_and_CG_Model.
4. MOUNTING METHODS
Fasteners:
Stainless steel bolts with locking nuts or thread-lock
Minimum 2-point mounting per component (4-point for batteries)
Vibration Isolation:
Rubber dampers or isolation mounts for battery and regulators
Foam padding for shock-sensitive components
Mounting Plates:
Aluminum or composite plates tied to chassis structure
Pre-drilled standardized hole patterns
5. THERMAL MANAGEMENT
Components must maintain safe operating temperatures:
Batteries: < 60°C
Regulators: < 80°C
Cooling strategies:
Passive airflow via vented panels
Thermal spacing between components (≥10 mm gap)
Heat sinks on regulators where required
Avoid mounting near high-heat sources (motors, power resistors).
6. ELECTRICAL ISOLATION
Non-conductive standoffs used where required
Insulation barriers between high-current components and chassis
Grounding points defined and consistent across system
No exposed terminals allowed in accessible areas
7. ACCESSIBILITY AND MAINTENANCE
Battery modules must be:
Removable without full disassembly
Accessible within ≤5 minutes
BMS and PDB must be:
Visible for inspection
Reachable for diagnostics
Main disconnect must be:
Clearly labeled
Accessible without tools
8. INTEGRATION WITH OTHER SYSTEMS
Cable Routing:
Direct routing paths from components to PDN
Strain relief applied at all connection points
Frame Architecture:
Mounting points aligned with structural load-bearing members
Deployment Profile:
Records exact component placement and configuration
FMEA References:
FM-003 (battery undervoltage)
FM-009 (power distribution fault)
9. VERIFICATION AND TESTING
Mechanical inspection:
Fastener torque verification
Mount stability under vibration
Thermal testing:
Full-load operation temperature checks
Electrical validation:
Continuity and isolation testing
Load distribution verification
All results documented per Configuration Management Plan
10. DESIGN INTENT
Ensure secure, stable, and safe mounting of all power components
Maintain optimal center of gravity and thermal performance
Enable rapid maintenance and modular upgrades
Prevent electrical faults due to vibration, heat, or poor isolation
Fully integrate with CM, FMEA, and deployment documentation
