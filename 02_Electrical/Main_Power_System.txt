1. PURPOSE

Define the main power architecture for OBSIDIAN-8 V3, including energy sources, distribution, protection, and system interfaces.

Ensures reliable and safe power delivery to all subsystems
Integrates with Energy Models (LiFePO4 / LiPo), FMEA, and Control Authority
Provides traceable configuration for deployment and CM records
2. SYSTEM OVERVIEW

The main power system consists of:

Battery modules (LiFePO4 or LiPo)
Battery Management System (BMS)
Power Distribution Network (PDN)
Voltage regulation stages
Protection and isolation mechanisms

Power flow:
Battery → BMS → Main Bus → Regulators → Subsystems

3. POWER SOURCES
Primary Source:
LiFePO4 (default, safety-focused deployments)
LiPo (high-performance deployments)
Configuration:
Single or parallel battery modules depending on mission profile
Hot-swappable modules supported
Selection Criteria:
LiFePO4 → long endurance, thermal stability
LiPo → high current burst capability
4. BATTERY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM (BMS)
Monitors:
Voltage (per cell and pack)
Current (charge/discharge)
Temperature
Functions:
Overcharge protection
Over-discharge protection
Overcurrent protection
Cell balancing
Interfaces with:
Mission Compute (telemetry)
Reactive Safety Layer (shutdown triggers)
5. POWER DISTRIBUTION NETWORK (PDN)
Main Bus Voltage:
12–14.6 V depending on battery type
Distribution Segments:
Segment	Voltage	Description
Actuator Bus	12V	High-current servo supply
Logic Bus	5V	Compute, controllers, sensors
Auxiliary Bus	3.3V	Low-power electronics
Routing:
Central backbone channels (per Frame Architecture)
Segregation of high-current and signal lines
6. VOLTAGE REGULATION
DC-DC converters used for:
12V → 5V (logic systems)
5V → 3.3V (low-power electronics)
Requirements:
Efficiency ≥ 90%
Thermal protection
Noise filtering for sensitive electronics
7. PROTECTION AND SAFETY
Hardware Protections:
Inline fuses or resettable breakers on main bus
Reverse polarity protection
Short-circuit protection
System Protections:
BMS-triggered shutdown on fault
Watchdog-triggered kill-line activation
Power isolation for faulty subsystems
Safety Priority Rule:
Power system must not interfere with kill-line or reactive safety functions
8. CONTROL INTEGRATION
Mission Compute:
Monitors power telemetry
Adjusts actuator load based on available energy
Reactive Safety Layer:
Triggers safe-stop under undervoltage or overcurrent conditions
System Control Authority Model:
Hardware power cutoff overrides all software commands
Ensures deterministic shutdown behavior
9. MAINTENANCE AND VERIFICATION
Pre-deployment checks:
Battery voltage and SOC
BMS status
Regulator output verification
Routine maintenance:
Inspect connectors and cables
Verify fuse integrity
Check thermal performance under load
All changes logged per Configuration Management Plan
10. DESIGN INTENT
Provide stable, efficient, and safe power delivery across all subsystems
Support both endurance and high-performance mission profiles
Ensure compatibility with energy models, FMEA, and deployment profiles
Maintain safety-first architecture with deterministic failure handling
Enable modular upgrades and traceable configuration changes
