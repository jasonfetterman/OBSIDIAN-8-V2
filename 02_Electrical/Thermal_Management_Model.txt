Thermal Management Model — REV B (16-Servo High-Current System)

OVERVIEW
This document defines thermal behavior, limits, and cooling strategy for OBSIDIAN-8 operating with 16 high-torque servos and peak currents exceeding 120A.

--------------------------------------------------

THERMAL SOURCES

Primary Heat Generators:

1. Servos (16x)
   - Internal motor + driver losses
   - Estimated heat under load: 10–25W per active servo

2. Power Distribution
   - Wiring resistive losses (I²R)
   - Connectors (XT90, terminals)

3. Voltage Regulation
   - Buck converter (7.4V → 5V)
   - Estimated loss: 5–10W

--------------------------------------------------

TOTAL THERMAL LOAD

Worst Case (high activity):
- Servos: 16 × 15W ≈ 240W
- Wiring + connectors: ~10–20W
- Buck converter: ~8W

Total System Heat:
- ~250–270W

This heat must be dissipated to prevent failure.

--------------------------------------------------

CRITICAL TEMPERATURE LIMITS

Servos:
- Safe operating: < 70°C case temperature
- Warning threshold: 60°C

Battery (LiPo):
- Safe: < 60°C
- Ideal: < 45°C

Wiring/Connectors:
- Must remain < 80°C

Buck Converter:
- < 85°C (with airflow)

--------------------------------------------------

COOLING STRATEGY

PASSIVE COOLING (BASELINE)

Frame Design:
- Open structure (no enclosed compartments)
- Air gaps around servo bodies

Mounting:
- Avoid trapping heat between servos and frame
- Allow airflow around all sides

Material:
- Aluminum frame acts as heat spreader

--------------------------------------------------

ACTIVE COOLING (REQUIRED)

Cooling Fans:

Quantity:
- 2 × 40mm or 60mm fans minimum

Placement:
1. Power distribution area (battery + wiring hub)
2. Central electronics deck (Pi + buck converter)

Specs:
- 5V or 7.4V fans
- Minimum 10 CFM each

Airflow Direction:
- Front-to-back or bottom-to-top
- Must create continuous airflow path

--------------------------------------------------

SERVO HEAT MANAGEMENT

Mounting:
- Do NOT fully enclose servos
- Allow direct exposure to airflow

Spacing:
- Minimum 5–10mm gap between adjacent servos

Optional:
- Aluminum mounting brackets for heat conduction

--------------------------------------------------

WIRING THERMAL CONTROL

High-current paths:
- Use 10–12 AWG (main)
- Use 14–16 AWG (distribution)

Rules:
- Avoid tight bundles of high-current wires
- Spread load paths where possible

Connectors:
- XT90 required (no XT60 at this load level)

--------------------------------------------------

BUCK CONVERTER COOLING

Requirements:
- Must include heatsink
- Must be placed in airflow path

Mounting:
- Elevated from surface (air gap underneath)

--------------------------------------------------

THERMAL MONITORING (IMPLEMENTED)

Use:
- thermal_monitor.cpp

Sensors (recommended):
- 1x near servo cluster
- 1x near battery
- 1x near buck converter

Trigger Thresholds:
- 60°C → reduce activity
- 70°C → initiate shutdown sequence

--------------------------------------------------

FAILURE MODES

Overheating causes:

- Servo torque loss
- Internal servo shutdown or failure
- Battery degradation or swelling
- Connector softening or failure

--------------------------------------------------

THERMAL TEST REQUIREMENTS

Must validate:

1. 5-minute continuous walk test
2. Peak load burst test
3. Idle heat soak test

Pass Criteria:
- No component exceeds defined limits
- No thermal shutdown occurs

--------------------------------------------------

DESIGN CONSTRAINTS

- System must dissipate up to 270W heat
- Active airflow is REQUIRED (not optional)
- No sealed enclosures allowed
- All critical components must be in airflow path
