1. PURPOSE

Define cable routing, strain relief, and electrical harness management for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Ensures safe operation of actuators, sensors, and power distribution.
Prevents mechanical wear, shorts, or signal interference.
Provides reference for assembly, maintenance, and CM documentation.
2. GENERAL GUIDELINES
All cables must follow predefined routing paths within the frame to avoid moving joints.
Maintain minimum bend radius per cable specification.
Use strain relief brackets and clamps at high-stress points.
Color-code cables for power, signal, and communication lines.
Avoid routing along edges or sharp corners.
Secure harnesses to maintain clearance from heat sources, actuators, and sensors.
3. POWER DISTRIBUTION CABLES
Cable Type	Routing Path	Strain Relief	Notes
Main battery bus	Chassis backbone, protected channels	Clamp every 0.25 m	High-current, shielded
Servo power lines	Embedded limb channels, away from moving joints	Cable loops at pivot points	Minimize torque stress
Sensor power lines	Side channels, routed along support panels	Velcro/tie mounts	Avoid EMI from power lines
4. SIGNAL AND COMMUNICATION CABLES
Cable Type	Routing Path	Strain Relief	Notes
ROS2 communication	Along frame edges, within conduits	Cable glands	Maintain signal integrity
Swarm network lines	Routed along central backbone, isolated from high-current lines	Tie mounts	Prevent interference
Maintenance/SSH lines	Accessible panels, short length	Loops with slack	Allow service without stress
5. STRAIN RELIEF METHODS
Cable Loops: Provide slack at joints to accommodate motion.
Grommets: Protect cables passing through panels.
Cable Clips/Brackets: Fixed at 0.25–0.3 m intervals.
Spiral Wrap / Braided Sleeving: Protect against abrasion.
Strain Relief at Connectors: Prevent pull on solder joints or connectors.
6. DYNAMIC AND ENVIRONMENTAL CONSIDERATIONS
Cables must tolerate continuous articulation of limbs and torso.
Maintain flexibility under -20°C to 60°C operational range.
Shielded cables used in high EMI zones (motors, power electronics).
Routing avoids water ingress, dust accumulation, and pinch points.
7. MAINTENANCE AND VERIFICATION
Visual inspection of harness integrity at scheduled maintenance intervals.
Verify strain relief attachment and bend radius compliance.
Confirm correct routing after any module replacement or repair.
Update Configuration Management records for any rerouted harness.
8. DESIGN INTENT
Ensure electrical reliability throughout lifecycle.
Protect actuators, sensors, and communication from mechanical and electrical stress.
Enable safe field maintenance without unintended damage.
Fully integrated with Frame Architecture, Actuated Limbs, and Deployment Profiles.
