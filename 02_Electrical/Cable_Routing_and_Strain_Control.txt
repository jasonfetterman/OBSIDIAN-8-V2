Cable Routing and Strain Control — REV B (High-Current Servo System)

OVERVIEW
This document defines cable routing, separation, and strain relief requirements for OBSIDIAN-8. The system must handle high-current servo wiring while protecting low-voltage signal integrity and preventing mechanical failure of connections.

--------------------------------------------------

DESIGN OBJECTIVES

- Ensure reliable power delivery under high current load
- Prevent electrical noise from affecting control signals
- Eliminate cable strain at connectors and solder joints
- Maintain serviceability and clean layout

--------------------------------------------------

CABLE CLASSIFICATION

All wiring is divided into three categories:

1. High-Current Power Cables
   - Servo power distribution (7.4V)
   - Battery leads

2. Logic Power Cables
   - Regulated 5V lines
   - Low-current distribution

3. Signal Cables
   - PWM control lines
   - Serial communication (UART/USB)
   - Sensor data lines

Each category must be routed separately.

--------------------------------------------------

ROUTING RULES

Separation:
- Maintain physical separation between power and signal cables
- Minimum spacing: 20–30 mm where possible

Crossing:
- If cables must cross, do so at 90 degrees

Bundling:
- Do NOT bundle high-current cables with signal lines
- Bundle signal lines separately using light cable management

Path Planning:
- Use fixed routing channels along the frame
- Avoid moving joints unless necessary

--------------------------------------------------

HIGH-CURRENT CABLING

Wire Gauge:
- Main battery leads: 12 AWG
- Servo distribution: 16–18 AWG

Requirements:
- Keep runs as short as possible
- Avoid sharp bends
- Use high-quality silicone-insulated wire

Connectors:
- XT60 or XT90 for battery connection
- Secure all connectors against vibration

--------------------------------------------------

SIGNAL INTEGRITY

PWM and Communication Lines:
- Keep short and direct
- Route away from servo power lines

Noise Reduction:
- Use twisted pair wiring for signal + ground where possible
- Optional shielding for longer runs

Ground Reference:
- Ensure solid common ground between Teensy and servo power system

--------------------------------------------------

STRAIN RELIEF

Critical Rule:
- No connector or solder joint should تحمل mechanical load

Implementation:
- Anchor cables to frame near connection points
- Use zip ties, clamps, or cable mounts
- Provide slack loops near moving joints

At Servos:
- Secure cable before it enters the servo housing
- Prevent pull force on connector pins

--------------------------------------------------

MOVING JOINTS

For articulated limbs:

- Use flexible, high-strand-count wire
- Provide controlled slack for full range of motion
- Route along joint axes where possible

Protection:
- Use braided sleeving or cable sheathing
- Prevent abrasion against frame edges

--------------------------------------------------

ABRASION AND PROTECTION

- Use grommets where cables pass through frame holes
- Avoid contact with sharp edges
- Add protective sleeving in high-risk areas

--------------------------------------------------

THERMAL CONSIDERATIONS

- Keep cables away from heat sources (regulators, motors)
- Ensure airflow around high-current wiring
- Do not enclose tightly bundled high-current wires

--------------------------------------------------

FAILURE MODES

Improper routing may cause:
- Electrical noise and signal corruption
- Intermittent connections
- Wire fatigue and breakage
- Connector failure under vibration

--------------------------------------------------

INSPECTION AND MAINTENANCE

Regular Checks:
- Inspect for worn insulation
- Check connector tightness
- Verify strain relief integrity

After Movement Testing:
- Re-check all moving joint cables
- Look for pinch points or rubbing

--------------------------------------------------

DESIGN CONSTRAINTS

- No exposed high-current conductors
- All wiring must be secured and supported
- Routing must allow easy access for maintenance
- Maintain clear separation of power and signal systems
