System Requirements Specification — REV C (Octopod Platform)

OVERVIEW
This document defines the functional, performance, and safety requirements for OBSIDIAN-8 as an eight-legged (octopod) robotic platform. All subsystems must comply with these requirements.

--------------------------------------------------

SYSTEM DESCRIPTION

OBSIDIAN-8 is an octopod robotic platform designed for high stability, load distribution, and controlled locomotion. The system uses high-torque servo actuation, distributed control, and onboard computation.

--------------------------------------------------

FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS

FR-1: Locomotion
- The system shall perform stable walking using an 8-leg configuration.

FR-2: Actuation Control
- The system shall control a minimum of 16 servo channels (2 DOF per leg: hip and knee).

FR-3: Command Interface
- The system shall accept motion commands from a high-level controller.

FR-4: Gait Execution
- The system shall execute coordinated multi-leg gait patterns.

FR-5: Emergency Shutdown
- The system shall provide immediate actuator shutdown via hardware kill switch.

--------------------------------------------------

PERFORMANCE REQUIREMENTS

PR-1: Load Capacity
- The system shall support a total mass of 8–15 kg.

PR-2: Stability
- The system shall maintain stability with a minimum of 4 legs in contact during motion.

PR-3: Response Time
- Actuator response shall occur within 50 ms.

PR-4: Motion Smoothness
- The system shall limit acceleration and velocity to prevent shock loading.

PR-5: Runtime
- The system shall operate for a minimum of 15 minutes under normal load.

--------------------------------------------------

ELECTRICAL REQUIREMENTS

ER-1: Power Architecture
- Separate servo and logic power rails are required.

ER-2: Current Handling
- The system shall support peak currents exceeding 120A due to increased servo count.

ER-3: Voltage Stability
- Logic power must remain stable under all servo load conditions.

ER-4: Protection
- The system shall include fused distribution and overcurrent protection.

--------------------------------------------------

MECHANICAL REQUIREMENTS

MR-1: Structural Integrity
- The frame shall withstand combined torque from all actuators without deformation.

MR-2: Joint Support
- All rotating joints shall be supported with bearings.

MR-3: Load Distribution
- The frame shall distribute load across multiple legs to reduce per-joint stress.

MR-4: Material Selection
- Load-bearing components shall use aluminum or steel.

--------------------------------------------------

CONTROL SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS

CR-1: Distributed Control
- A microcontroller (Teensy) shall handle real-time actuator control.

CR-2: Communication
- Reliable communication between compute layers is required.

CR-3: Motion Planning
- The system shall enforce velocity and acceleration limits.

CR-4: Gait Coordination
- The system shall coordinate leg groups to maintain stability.

CR-5: Watchdog
- The system shall detect and respond to control signal loss.

--------------------------------------------------

GAIT REQUIREMENTS

GR-1: Multi-Leg Coordination
- The system shall use staggered leg movement to maintain continuous support.

GR-2: Stability Margin
- At least 50% of legs shall remain in contact during normal walking.

GR-3: Load Balancing
- Load shall be distributed across multiple legs to reduce peak torque.

--------------------------------------------------

SAFETY REQUIREMENTS

SR-1: Kill Switch
- A hardware emergency stop shall immediately disable servo power.

SR-2: Fail-Safe State
- The system shall default to a safe state on failure.

SR-3: Power Isolation
- Servo and logic systems shall be independently controlled.

SR-4: Fault Handling
- The system shall prevent uncontrolled motion during faults.

--------------------------------------------------

ENVIRONMENTAL REQUIREMENTS

EN-1: Operating Conditions
- Indoor operation under controlled conditions.

EN-2: Thermal Limits
- All components shall operate within safe temperature ranges.

--------------------------------------------------

TESTING REQUIREMENTS

TR-1: Validation
- The system shall pass all defined movement and safety tests.

TR-2: Repeatability
- The system shall perform consistently across multiple cycles.

--------------------------------------------------

DESIGN CONSTRAINTS

- The system shall not exceed actuator torque limits during operation.
- High-current and signal wiring must remain isolated.
- Stability shall be prioritized over speed in default configuration.
