1. PRIMARY STRUCTURE — 3030 CONNECTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

**Primary Ring Joints (3030 ↔ 3030)**  
Fastener Type: M8 Socket Head Cap Screw  
Grade: 10.9 (minimum)  
Length: 25 mm (re‑verify with corner block depth)  
Washer: M8 hardened steel  
Thread Interface: Steel T‑nut / threaded insert block  
Torque Spec: 22 – 25 N·m  
Washer Option: Nord‑Lock type recommended for vibration‑sensitive joints  

**Vertical Post ↔ Ring Node**  
Fastener Type: M8 Socket Head Cap Screw  
Grade: 10.9 (minimum)  
Length: 25 – 30 mm  
Thread Interface: Steel T‑nut (compatible profile)  
Torque Spec: 22 – 25 N·m  
Apply medium strength threadlocker (blue).  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. SECONDARY STRUCTURE — 2020 CONNECTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

**2020 ↔ 3030 Interfaces**  
Fastener Type: M6 Socket Head Cap Screw  
Grade: 10.9 (minimum)  
Length: 16 – 20 mm  
Thread Interface: Steel T‑nut (slot compatible)  
Torque Spec: 9 – 11 N·m  

**2020 Cross Bracing**  
Fastener Type: M6 Socket Head Cap Screw  
Grade: 10.9 (minimum)  
Length: 16 – 20 mm  
Torque Spec: 9 – 11 N·m  
Printed alignment brackets provide lateral stability only — not primary load transfer.  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. PRINTED CF NYLON COMPONENT INTERFACES
------------------------------------------------------------

Heat‑Set Inserts:  
- Type: Brass heat‑set threaded insert  
- Sizes: M6 and M8 (common)  
- Install Method: Controlled temperature insertion (220 – 240 °C)  
- Minimum Wall Thickness: ≥ 3 mm radial (≥ 4 mm for high‑torque interfaces)  

Bolt Hole Oversize: + 0.2 mm over nominal diameter  

Rules for Printed Interfaces:  
- No direct threading into nylon or PETG material.  
- All structural fasteners require threaded inserts.  
- Avoid tensile loading through printed walls.  
- Reinforce or shim at primary torque zones (Femur/Coxa mounts).  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. VIBRATION AND THREAD CONTROL
------------------------------------------------------------

Threadlocker: Medium strength  (Blue Loctite 243 or equivalent) applied to all structural fasteners.  
Nord‑Lock washers installed on Primary Ring Joints and Vertical Post connections.  
Rubber / polymer washers allowed only at non‑structural mounts (electronics or covers).  
Torque sequence: cross‑pattern starting from center outward to minimize stress concentration.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. GENERAL HARDWARE RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Use only metric hardware; no imperial substitutes approved.  
- Minimum grade for primary load paths = 10.9; no grade 8.8 or lower fasteners permitted.  
- Re‑torque all structural fasteners after initial load validation / first walk test.  
- Inspect and re‑verify torque on 3030 ↔ printed junctions after Stair‑Mode and Tripod Gait tests.  
- Check printed inserts and brackets for creep or deformation after first 10 operational hours.  
- Replace fasteners if any sign of corrosion, rounding, or thread galling.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. TRACEABILITY AND PROCUREMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

All hardware lots shall include manufacturer certification per ISO 898‑1 and ISO 3506.  
Lot numbers and quantities recorded under /10_Lifecycle/Hardware_Register.txt.  
Substitutions require Engineering Change Request (Class B or higher).  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Provide a consistent fastener baseline for OBSIDIAN‑8 V3 hybrid structure ensuring:  
- Uniform strength and vibration resistance across all metal and printed interfaces.  
- Predictable torque‑to‑clamp relationships for FEA validation.  
- Service‑safe assembly with repeatable load test performance.  
- Full traceability for procurement and maintenance records under Configuration Management.
