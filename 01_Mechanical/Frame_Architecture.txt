1. PURPOSE

Define the mechanical frame architecture of OBSIDIAN-8 V3, including structural design, load distribution, material selection, and modularity for actuators, sensors, and armor systems.

This document ensures:

Predictable mechanical behavior under operational loads
Compatibility with actuation and armor subsystems
Modularity for maintenance and upgrades
Traceable design decisions linked to CM records
2. FRAME STRUCTURE OVERVIEW
Main Chassis: Central backbone connecting all subsystems. Material: High-strength aluminum alloy or carbon-fiber composite.
Leg Attachments: Modular connection points for articulated limbs (Leg_MK1_Obsidian, Leg_MK2_Obsidian).
Torso Modules: Houses Mission Compute, Battery System, Sensors.
Armor Mounting Points: Standardized slots for Covers_armor components.
Payload Bays: Modular compartments for mission-specific equipment.
Maintenance Access: Removable panels for internal inspections without disassembly.
3. MATERIAL SELECTION AND PROPERTIES
Component	Material	Notes / Reasoning
Main Chassis	7075 Aluminum Alloy	High strength-to-weight ratio
Limb Joints	Titanium Alloy	Fatigue-resistant, corrosion-proof
Armor Covers	Polycarbonate + Kevlar	Lightweight impact protection
Internal Panels	ABS Plastic	Easy to remove, low cost
Fasteners	Stainless Steel	Corrosion resistance, reliability
4. LOAD AND STRESS CONSIDERATIONS
Frame designed for dynamic motion loads under full articulation.
Safety factor ≥ 2.0 for all structural elements.
Stress distribution analyzed via FEA (Finite Element Analysis) on:
Chassis backbone
Limb mounting points
Armor attachment interfaces
Vibration damping integrated at actuator mounts to protect electronics.
5. MODULARITY AND INTERFACES
Actuator Interfaces: Standardized mounting holes, torque limits documented.
Sensor Mounts: Adjustable brackets with orientation locking.
Armor Panels: Quick-release mechanism, keyed alignment to frame.
Power & Data Channels: Embedded routing paths within frame for wiring harnesses.
6. COMPATIBILITY WITH CM / DEPLOYMENT
Frame revision recorded in Configuration Management Plan.
Each unit linked to its deployment_profile specifying hardware revision.
Structural changes require Change Request (CR) approval, per CM guidelines.
7. DESIGN INTENT

The frame architecture ensures:

High structural integrity while maintaining weight efficiency
Modular and upgradeable mechanical layout
Integration compatibility with actuation, sensor, and armor subsystems
Predictable performance across all operational scenarios
Traceable and auditable design evolution
