1. PURPOSE

Define the mechanical frame architecture of OBSIDIAN-8 V3 using a hybrid extrusion and 3D printed design. Includes structural layout, load paths, material selection, modular interfaces, and integration with actuators, sensors, and armor systems.

This document ensures:

Predictable mechanical behavior under operational loads
Compatibility with 3D printed subsystems and armor
Modularity for maintenance, upgrades, and design iteration
Traceable design decisions linked to CM records

2. FRAME STRUCTURE OVERVIEW

Main Chassis: 
- Backbone constructed from 3030 aluminum extrusions forming the central torso structure. 
- Primary load-bearing paths for battery, mission compute, and limb attachments.

Secondary Supports:
- 2020 extrusions for cross-braces, sensor mounts, and payload bay frames. 
- Provides structural rigidity while minimizing weight.

Leg Attachments:
- Modular 3D printed interface brackets attach limbs to 3030 backbone. 
- Compatible with Leg_MK1_Obsidian and Leg_MK2_Obsidian designs.
- Reinforced gussets and brackets used for high-stress points.

Torso Modules:
- 3D printed housings for Mission Compute, Battery System, and Sensors.
- Designed to slide or bolt onto T-slot extrusions for easy access.

Armor Mounting Points:
- 3D printed quick-release brackets attach to standardized extrusion slots.
- Allows modular armor panels (Covers_armor) to be replaced or upgraded.

Payload Bays:
- 3D printed compartments fit within or attach to extrusion framework.
- Modular and configurable based on mission requirements.

Maintenance Access:
- Panels and modules designed for slide-fit or bolted removal.
- No disassembly of backbone required for standard maintenance.

3. MATERIAL SELECTION AND PROPERTIES

Component         Material / Method           Notes / Reasoning
Main Chassis      3030 Aluminum Extrusions   High stiffness, lightweight, modular
Secondary Supports 2020 Aluminum Extrusions  Cross-bracing, sensor mounts, low weight
Limb Brackets      3D Printed PETG/Nylon    Strength at actuator mounts, customizable
Armor Covers       3D Printed + Polycarbonate Infill or hybrid for impact resistance
Internal Panels    3D Printed ABS or PETG   Lightweight, removable
Fasteners          M5/M6 Bolts + T-slot Nuts Standardized modular attachment

4. LOAD AND STRESS CONSIDERATIONS

- Backbone extrusions designed for dynamic motion and static loads.
- Safety factor ≥ 2.0 for all extrusion members and printed actuator mounts.
- High-stress points (leg mounts, shoulder joints) reinforced with printed gussets and metal brackets.
- Finite Element Analysis (FEA) recommended on main 3030 chassis and key printed components.
- Vibration damping integrated via printed gaskets or rubber pads at actuator mounts.

5. MODULARITY AND INTERFACES

Actuator Interfaces:
- 3D printed mounting plates bolt to extrusion end plates.
- Adjustable slots for fine alignment.
- Torque limits documented per actuator type.

Sensor Mounts:
- 2020 extrusion-mounted sliding brackets with locking tabs.
- Allows orientation adjustment and rapid replacement.

Armor Panels:
- Quick-release printed brackets align to extrusion slots.
- Panels can be swapped without tools for field maintenance.

Power & Data Channels:
- Internal extrusion cavities route wiring harnesses.
- Printed cable guides clip onto extrusions for secure routing.

6. COMPATIBILITY WITH CM / DEPLOYMENT

- Frame revision recorded in Configuration Management Plan.
- Each unit linked to deployment_profile specifying hardware and printed part revisions.
- Structural or interface changes require Change Request (CR) approval per CM guidelines.

7. DESIGN INTENT

- Maintain high structural integrity with minimal weight using extrusion backbone.
- Modular, upgradeable layout compatible with 3D printed limbs, sensors, and armor.
- Predictable performance under dynamic loads and articulation.
- Fully traceable and auditable design evolution.
