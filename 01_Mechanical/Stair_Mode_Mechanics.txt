1. PURPOSE

Define mechanical and kinematic behavior of OBSIDIAN-8 V3 for stair traversal.

Ensures stable motion while ascending or descending stairs.
Integrates with control authority, reactive safety, and neutral stance geometry.
Provides reference for calibration, testing, and deployment.
2. MODE ACTIVATION CRITERIA
Stair mode engages when:
Front leg detects step elevation > 0.05 m (via LIDAR or proximity sensor)
Incline exceeds normal ramp threshold
Stable support polygon confirmed
System automatically exits stair mode when:
Step elevation < 0.02 m for >1 second
Uneven terrain ends or neutral stance regained
3. LEG KINEMATICS ADJUSTMENTS
Joint	Nominal Angle	Stair Ascend Adjustment	Stair Descend Adjustment	Notes
Hip	0°	+10°	-10°	Increases step height
Knee	30°	+20°	+10°	Avoids scraping step
Ankle	0°	+5°	-5°	Foot angle to step surface
Adjustments ensure foot clearance and stability while keeping CG within safe bounds.
Step-by-step motion is coordinated with reactive controller to prevent tipping.
4. LOAD AND STABILITY CONSIDERATIONS
Each leg must bear up to 40% dynamic load during step transitions.
CG dynamically shifted toward grounded legs during ascent and descent.
Limbs follow predefined support polygon: at least 3 points of contact for stability.
Torque limits enforced per actuator specifications to prevent overloading.
5. ARMOR AND PAYLOAD CLEARANCE
Leg retraction angles calculated to avoid contact with armor covers.
Payload and torso modules maintain center-of-mass alignment with neutral stance adjustments.
Quick collision checks via embedded sensors before step lift.
6. CONTROL INTEGRATION
Reactive controller monitors:
Step height & incline sensors
Ground reaction forces
Limb position sensors
Control authority hierarchy:
Hardware kill-line
Reactive safety preemption
Mission compute / stair mode sequence
Swarm commands cannot override stair-mode adjustments.
7. VALIDATION AND TESTING
Simulation: Multi-step ascent/descent with varying payloads.
Field: Stair traversal at 0.2 m, 0.3 m, and 0.4 m step heights.
Metrics recorded: torque utilization, CG deviation, actuator temperature, slip incidents.
FMEA cross-reference: FM-003 (battery undervoltage), FM-004 (servo overheating), FM-008 (IMU drift).
8. DESIGN INTENT
Provides repeatable, safe stair traversal for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.
Maintains structural integrity, actuator safety, and balance.
Fully integrated with Neutral Stance Geometry, Mass and CG Model, and System Control Authority Model.
Ensures operator and swarm commands respect reactive safety hierarchy during stair mode.
