1. PURPOSE

Define the mass properties and center of gravity (CG) model for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.
This ensures:

Stability under operational motion
Proper load distribution across limbs and actuators
Accurate simulation for control and safety systems
Traceability for CM, FMEA, and deployment profiles

3. CENTER OF GRAVITY (CG) MODEL
Origin: Geometric center of main chassis reference frame.
CG Location (Nominal, XYZ):
X: 0.0 m (longitudinal center)
Y: 0.0 m (lateral center)
Z: 0.45 m (height from chassis base)
CG Sensitivity:
Payload addition ±0.1 m
Armor variation ±0.05 m
Battery placement ±0.02 m
CG Considerations:
Legs designed to support >120% of static weight
Torso and payload mass concentrated near CG to minimize torque on actuators
Armor and add-ons designed symmetrically to maintain lateral balance
4. DYNAMIC MASS CONSIDERATIONS
Actuation during motion changes effective CG:
Forward/Back Leg Extension: ±0.05 m X-axis
Lateral Swing: ±0.03 m Y-axis
Limb Elevation: ±0.02 m Z-axis
Software control loops use this dynamic CG to:
Adjust torque in real-time
Maintain stability in reactive controller
Feed FMEA risk models for motion limits
5. MODULARITY AND CONFIGURATION
Each subsystem mass tracked in Configuration Management Plan.
Changes in battery, armor, or payload must update deployment_profile with new mass/CG data.
Supports rapid recalculation for mission-specific loadouts.
6. DESIGN INTENT
Maintain predictable stability and actuation performance under all operational configurations.
Ensure safe operation with both static and dynamic loads.
Integrate with FMEA, CM, and System Control Authority to prevent tipping, overload, or actuator faults.
Provide accurate data for simulation, mission planning, and control authority validation.
