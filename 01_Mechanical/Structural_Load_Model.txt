1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define the mechanical load distribution and structural integrity model for OBSIDIAN‑8 V3.4 (hybrid 3030/2020 aluminum backbone with 3D‑printed modules and limbs).  

This document ensures:
- Frame and actuators sustain static, dynamic, and impact loads without structural failure.  
- Serves as analysis reference for FEA, Configuration Management (CM), FMEA, and deployment validation.  
- Supports normal terrain locomotion, stair traversal, and payload operations.  

Related References: /01_Mechanical/Frame_Architecture.txt  /01_Mechanical/Mass_and_CG_Model.txt  /01_Mechanical/Stair_Mode_Mechanics.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. LOAD TYPES
------------------------------------------------------------

| Load Type | Description | Notes |
|:--|:--|:--|
| Static Load | Weight of chassis, payload, battery, and armor at rest | Base design load ≈ 26 kg total |
| Dynamic Load | Forces during walking and acceleration / deceleration | Up to 1.5 × static load |
| Impact Load | Sudden terrain impact or obstacle collision | Safety Factor ≥ 2.0 |
| Torsional Load | Twist of frame due to uneven support or tripod stance | Evaluated per actuator mount |
| Bending Load | Beam bending in extrusions and limb mounts | Assessed via FEA (deflection ≤ limit)|

------------------------------------------------------------
3. LOAD DISTRIBUTION
------------------------------------------------------------

- **Legs:** Each leg supports ≈ 35‑40 % of static mass in neutral stance; printed gussets reduce localized stress.  
- **Torso Modules:** Battery and compute payload distributed evenly along X‑axis to minimize torsion.  
- **Armor Panels:** Light polycarbonate hybrids reduce lateral asymmetry in mass loading.  
- **Actuators:** Torque monitoring limits ensure operation ≤ 90 % of rated continuous output.  
- **Support Polygon:** Cg remains inside contact polygon under all expected motion states to avoid tipping.  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. SAFETY FACTORS AND MARGINS
------------------------------------------------------------

- **Material Factor of Safety (FoS):** ≥ 2.0 for all primary extrusion and bracket members.  
- **Joint Stress Limit:** ≤ 80 % of material yield under maximum simulated load.  
- **Deflection Limits:**  
 • 3030 backbone deflection ≤ 1.5 mm under max static load.  
 • Limb plates / brackets ≤ 2 mm under max dynamic load.  
 • Printed brackets include reinforced gusset geometry validated in FEA mesh tests.  

Quality metrics recorded under /09_Testing/Structural_Validation_Report.txt.

5. LOAD CASES
------------------------------------------------------------

| Case ID | Scenario | Max Load (kg) | Notes |
|:--|:--|:--:|:--|
| LC‑001 | Neutral stance (static) | 26 | Base Cg (V3.4 mass) |
| LC‑002 | Forward motion (flat terrain) | 39 | Dynamic torque included |
| LC‑003 | Stair ascent (full payload) | 42 | Step height 0.3 m; body pitch ≤ 10 ° |
| LC‑004 | Stair descent (full payload) | 42 | Negative pitch; braking torque profile |
| LC‑005 | Frontal collision (remedial impact) | 56 | Simulated impact force with FoS 2.0 |
| LC‑006 | Tripod gait transition (uneven terrain) | 36 | Torsional frame twist evaluation |

All cases analyzed using FEA (SolidWorks / Ansys) with 10 mm element mesh on main 3030 segments and printed mounts.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- Preserve structural integrity across static, dynamic, and impact conditions.  
- Maintain predictable elastic response for control and stability systems.  
- Prevent component overload via actuator and joint torque limits.  
- Enable accurate simulation inputs for Reactive Safety and Control Authority.  
- Provide full traceability within CM and Design Change Records.  
- Support serviceable hybrid frame architecture for rapid module replacement and upgrades.
