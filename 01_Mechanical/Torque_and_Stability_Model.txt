OBSIDIAN-8 V3 – TORQUE AND STABILITY MODEL
Revision: V3.3
Status: Authoritative

------------------------------------------------------------
1. SYSTEM MASS ASSUMPTION
------------------------------------------------------------

Target Total Mass:
24.8 kg (hybrid frame + printed limbs)

Gravitational Force:
F = m × g
F = 24.8 kg × 9.81 m/s²
F ≈ 243 N total system load

------------------------------------------------------------
2. STATIC TRIPOD SUPPORT MODEL
------------------------------------------------------------

Tripod gait:
3 legs support full system weight.

Load per supporting leg:

243 N ÷ 3 ≈ 81 N per leg

Dynamic factor (movement, acceleration, terrain):
Apply 2× safety multiplier.

Design Load per Leg:
≈ 162 N

------------------------------------------------------------
3. TIBIA TORQUE REQUIREMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Assume tibia effective length:
0.18 m (slightly shorter due to hybrid frame geometry)

Torque required at joint:

τ = F × L
τ = 162 N × 0.18 m
τ ≈ 29 Nm required per tibia joint

------------------------------------------------------------
4. SERVO CAPABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------

JX PDI-HV2060MG:
60 kg·cm @ 7.4V

Convert:
1 kg·cm ≈ 0.098 Nm
60 kg·cm ≈ 5.88 Nm

Conclusion:

Single servo torque insufficient for 29 Nm load
if acting alone at full lever arm.

------------------------------------------------------------
5. DESIGN IMPLICATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Stability achieved by:

- Geometry optimization
- Reducing effective moment arm
- Distributing load through femur + coxa
- Maintaining CG inside support triangle (now ~0.42 m Z)
- Avoiding worst-case lever extension

Servo never operates at full extension under load.

------------------------------------------------------------
6. STAIR MODE TORQUE LOAD
------------------------------------------------------------

Worst-case stair ascent:

Front tripod supports majority load.  
CG shifts forward due to hybrid frame and battery placement.

Estimated load multiplier:
2.5× static load.

Design worst-case per leg:
≈ 203–210 N

Torque at 0.18 m effective lever:
τ ≈ 37–38 Nm

System must avoid static hold at full extension.

------------------------------------------------------------
7. STABILITY POLYGON MODEL
------------------------------------------------------------

CG projection must remain within:

Tripod support triangle.

Tilt threshold:
≤ 10° sustained during stair climb.

Abort if:
CG approaches triangle edge.

------------------------------------------------------------
8. DESIGN SAFETY MARGIN
------------------------------------------------------------

System does NOT rely on servo stall torque.

Instead relies on:

- Shortened moment arm during load  
- Controlled stride length  
- Reduced aggression under low voltage  
- Stair mode frequency reduction  

Safety multiplier incorporated in control logic.

------------------------------------------------------------
9. FUTURE UPGRADE PATH
------------------------------------------------------------

If torque margin insufficient:

- Upgrade to 80–100 kg·cm class servos  
- Recalculate torque envelope accordingly  
- Reinforce printed brackets at high-torque points

------------------------------------------------------------
10. ENGINEERING SIGNATURE
------------------------------------------------------------

Torque modeling is:

- Conservative  
- Dynamic-factor aware  
- Not stall-dependent  
- Geometry-aware  
- Energy-aware  

Movement is controlled to stay within envelope
