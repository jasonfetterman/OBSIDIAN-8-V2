1. CORE DIMENSIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Standing Height (Neutral): 1143 mm  
Crouch Height (reference Cg): ≈ 680 mm  
Target Mass (operational): ≤ 20 kg frame only  
Max Stair Rise: 200 mm  

Body Configuration: Hybrid 3030 aluminum extrusion backbone + 2020 secondary supports in octagonal layout.  
3D‑printed modules provide armor, limb interfaces, and payload mounts.  
Design intent anchored to /01_Mechanical/Frame_Architecture.txt.  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. BODY STRUCTURE
------------------------------------------------------------

Frame Type:  
- 3030 aluminum extrusion forming primary load paths.  
- 2020 cross‑braces support sensors and payload bays.  
- 3D‑printed brackets mount actuators, panels, and electronics.  

Central Electronics Plane:  
- Diameter ≈ 300 mm; 3 mm aluminum or 5 mm polycarbonate plate.  
- Mounted to 3030 backbone via T‑slot nuts and insulated spacers.  

Leg Mounting:  
- Reinforced printed brackets with integrated gussets.  
- M6 fasteners clamp to 3030 backbone for high torque loads.  

Structural Goal:  
- Minimize torsional flex under tripod operation.  
- Preserve modularity for rapid maintenance and upgrade cycles.  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. LEG GEOMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------

Configuration: 3 DOF per leg (Coxa / Femur / Tibia).  
Coxa — reinforced printed bracket with embedded bearing boss.  
Femur — printed section with internal ribbing for torsion resistance.  
Tibia — extended segment for stair clearance.  

Design Bias:  
- Long tibia for stair reach.  
- Moderate femur for torque efficiency.  
- Coxa width optimized for lateral stability.  
- Metal thread inserts in printed parts at attachment points.  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. NEUTRAL STANCE GEOMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------

Neutral stance must achieve:
- Foot placement defining maximum stability polygon.  
- Cg within ± 50 mm of geometric center.  
- No servo operating near stall torque.  
- Tilt ≤ 5° at neutral.  

Stance polygon fully encloses Cg projection; backbone and brackets maintain alignment under 10 N‑m torsion.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. TRIPOD GAIT GEOMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------

Tripod Sets for six‑leg pattern:  
Set A = LF, RM, LR  Set B = RF, LM, RR  

Lift Height – Normal Mode: ≈ 80 – 120 mm  
Lift Height – Stair Mode: ≈ 200 mm  
Stride Length – Scaled to voltage / mode ratio.  

Design Rules:  
- Cg projection shall remain inside active tripod triangle.  
- Frame deflection ≤ 1 mm under dynamic load.  
- Reactive controller monitors leg feedback for stability.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. STAIR MODE GEOMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------

Front Tripod lift arc ≥ 200 mm.  
Body pitch compensation applies pre‑rear tripod lift via energy model.  
Max sustained pitch angle = 10° (verified per Operational_Envelope.txt).  

Abort conditions:  
- Excessive tilt > 10°  
- Current > actuator threshold  
- Voltage sag detected by BMS  
Trigger → Reactive STOP and Safe‑Pose lock.  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. HEAD MODULE GEOMETRY
------------------------------------------------------------

Mount Position: forward‑biased above Cg plane on front 3030 rail.  

Contains:  
- 6 RGB cameras for 360° coverage  
- 1 depth camera  
- IMU  
- RTK antenna (top surface, sky exposure)  

Cable Routing: strain‑relieved through extrusion channels and printed guides;
secured with nylon loom and isolation pads against vibration.  

------------------------------------------------------------
8. CENTER OF GRAVITY TARGET MODEL
------------------------------------------------------------

Primary Cg target within central octagonal 3030 core.  
Vertical Cg position: as low as practical while preserving stair clearance.  

Battery Placement → lowest practical plane (printed housing integrated to backbone).  
Compute Modules → centered on electronics plate for symmetry and thermal balance.  

Cg validation method defined in /00_System_Definition/Mass_and_Center_of_Gravity_Model.txt.  

------------------------------------------------------------
9. DESIGN CONSTRAINTS
------------------------------------------------------------

- Structural flex shall not exceed values that disturb SLAM alignment.  
- No servo horn is load‑bearing without metal reinforcement.  
- Primary torque‑transfer joints use bearing‑supported shafts.  
- Printed parts must not carry primary torsional loads.  
- All printed brackets fastened to extrusions with M5/M6 hardware and embedded gussets.  
- All geometry updates require CR review and FEA verification.  

------------------------------------------------------------
10. DESIGN PRIORITIES
------------------------------------------------------------

1. Stability under load  
2. Predictable Cg behavior  
3. Stair safety and tripod control  
4. Cable and thermal protection  
5. Ease of service / rapid module exchange  
6. Validated hybrid integration of extrusion backbone and printed components  
7. Compliance with FMEA and Operational_Envelope standards
