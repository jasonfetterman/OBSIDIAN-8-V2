------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define the central core mounting geometry and load path for the OBSIDIAN‑8 V3.4 hybrid frame.  
This section governs the structural integration of the propulsion spine, avionics core, power system, and mission payload stack within the reserved central volume.

Core assembly requirements:
- Remain within reserved clearance zone  
- Tie directly into 3030 main ring and radial 2020 braces  
- Avoid loading 3D‑printed parts in tension or primary shear roles  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. RESERVED CORE VOLUME
------------------------------------------------------------

Central Clearance Cylinder:  
- Diameter = 300 mm  
- Height = Full FRAME_HEIGHT (≈ 1143 mm)  

No permanent structure may extend outside this boundary without documented Change Request approval.  
Volume preserved for wiring and airflow service routes.  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. CORE SUPPORT STRATEGY
------------------------------------------------------------

**Primary Support:** six radial 2020 braces anchored to the lower 3030 ring plane.  
**Secondary Support (optional):** upper radial tie‑ins at 60–65 % of frame height (~700 mm plane).  

Core must never hang solely from upper ring attachments.  
All fastener points must permit shear transfer into radial members.  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. LOWER CORE PLATE
------------------------------------------------------------

Mount Type: Circular or hexagonal plate  
Recommended Diameter: 220–260 mm  
Material: 6–8 mm aluminum plate or equivalent composite  
Mounting: Bolted directly to radial 2020 braces using M6 fasteners.  

Printed gussets may aid lateral alignment but shall not carry vertical load.  
Apply thread‑locking compound and torque to 8 N·m minimum.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. UPPER CORE STABILIZER
------------------------------------------------------------

Optional upper stabilizer ring installed at ≈ 700 mm height to vertical post nodes.  
Purpose: limit lateral oscillation of central stack and dampen torsional vibrations.  
Material: 3 mm aluminum / CF composite strip ring with four M5 anchors.  

Removal of the stabilizer requires Mechanical Change Request (Class B minimum).  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. LOAD PATH
------------------------------------------------------------

Core Mass  
→ Lower Core Plate  
→ Radial 2020 Braces  
→ 3030 Ring Nodes  
→ Vertical Posts  

No single radial brace may carry > 25 % of total core mass.  
Mass distribution must remain symmetrical around center axis.  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. MASS LIMITS
------------------------------------------------------------

Maximum recommended central core mass: 20–25 kg.  
If exceeded, install secondary reinforcement ring or additional radial braces per Structural_Load_Model.txt.  
Design FoS ≥ 2.0 for core mount members.  

------------------------------------------------------------
8. FASTENER RULES
------------------------------------------------------------

| Interface | Specification | Notes |
|:--|:--|:--|
| Core‑to‑brace | M6 Grade 10.9 | All primary bolt joints |
| Bolt spacing | ≥ 40 mm center‑to‑center | Prevents cantilever stress |
| Fastener torque | 8 – 10 N·m | Apply medium threadlocker |
| Printed brackets | Alignment only | No load‑bearing application |

Cantilever mounts using single bolts are prohibited.  

------------------------------------------------------------
9. VIBRATION CONTROL
------------------------------------------------------------

- Moderate vibration isolators may be used between electronics modules and plate.  
- Do **not** isolate entire core mass from the frame structure.  
- Damping goal: attenuation ≈ 30 % at 50–150 Hz response band.  
- Comply with Vibration Validation Plan (/09_Testing/Structural_Validation_Report.txt).  

------------------------------------------------------------
10. CENTER OF GRAVITY CONTROL
------------------------------------------------------------

- Heavy components (batteries) shall mount below 50 % frame height ( ≤ 570 mm ).  
- Compute modules and payload processors centralized near neutral Cg point.  
- Avoid tall narrow mass stacks that shift Cg beyond ± 25 mm from origin.  

Mass placement verified against /01_Mechanical/Mass_and_CG_Model.txt.  

------------------------------------------------------------
11. ALIGNMENT REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------------------------------

- Core plate center aligned within ± 2 mm of geometric origin.  
- Vertical alignment tolerance < 1°.  
- Radial brace deflection ≤ 1 mm under nominal load (FEA validated).  

Alignment verified using laser reference or coordinate measurement probe.  

------------------------------------------------------------
12. FAILURE CONDITIONS
------------------------------------------------------------

Redesign and re‑qualification required if:  
- Core oscillates visibly under torsion testing > 1° peak to peak.  
- Radial braces deflect > 3 mm under static load.  
- Mount bolts loosen after dynamic vibration test.  
- Printed clamps show creep > 0.5 mm or surface fracture.  

------------------------------------------------------------
13. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Central Core Mount design preserves:
- Rigid integration between core modules and structural frame.  
- Predictable vibration response and resonance isolation without sacrificing accuracy.  
- Safe load path distribution through metal extrusions instead of printed components.  
- Repeatable assembly geometry traceable under Configuration Management principles.
