------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
------------------------------------------------------------

Define dimensional tolerances and manufacturing controls for aluminum extrusions, printed components, and assembly alignment within the 1143 mm (45 in) hybrid frame.  
Goal — prevent cumulative tolerance drift that degrades rigidity and fitment precision.  

Linked Documents: Frame_Cut_List_v3.txt, Printed_Parts_Specification_v1.txt, Test & Validation Master Plan_v1.txt  

------------------------------------------------------------
2. ALUMINUM CUT TOLERANCES
------------------------------------------------------------

| Cut Type | Parameter | Tolerance | Notes |
|:--|:--|:--:|:--|
| Linear Cuts (3030 / 2020) | Length | ± 0.5 mm | Applies to all field and shop cuts |
| Miter Cut (15 °) | Angle | ± 0.5 ° | Verify pair fit before fastening |
| Miter Cut (15 °) | Length | ± 0.5 mm | Maintain matched segment sets |
| Vertical Posts | Height | 1143 mm ± 0.5 mm | Across all eight posts |

Segments must be paired and assembled in matched sets as produced. Do not mix unmatched tolerance parts between rings.  

------------------------------------------------------------
3. RING DIAMETER TOLERANCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Primary Ring Outer Diameter = 1143 mm ± 1.0 mm  
Measurement = average of three diameters at 120 ° intervals.  

Out‑of‑round condition ≤ 2.0 mm total (deviation between largest/smallest).  
Ring flatness verified before vertical post installation.  

------------------------------------------------------------
4. VERTICAL ALIGNMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

| Parameter | Tolerance | Notes |
|:--|:--:|:--|
| Post Vertical Deviation | < 0.5 ° | Use digital level or laser alignment tool |
| Post Height Variation | < 1.0 mm | Referenced against datum plate |
| Upper Ring Concentricity | ± 2 mm | Relative to primary ring center |

All posts must seat flush and square. Shim only beneath metal bases — never beneath printed interfaces.  

------------------------------------------------------------
5. 2020 BRACING TOLERANCES
------------------------------------------------------------

- Radial brace length field‑trimmed after node confirmation; final installed tolerance ± 1 mm.  
- Cross braces cut after primary structure confirmed square.  
- Ensure braces engage securely to 3030 main ring using corner brackets.  
- Printed gussets may provide lateral support only — not primary load reinforcement.  

------------------------------------------------------------
6. PRINTED PART TOLERANCES
------------------------------------------------------------

| Parameter | Tolerance | Notes |
|:--|:--:|:--|
| Dimensional (after shrink compensation) | ± 0.3 mm | CF Nylon or PETG per spec |
| Bolt Hole Oversize | +0.2 mm | Facilitates insert fit |
| Insert Seating Depth | ± 0.2 mm | Flush to  –0.1 mm |
| Mating Surface Flatness | ≤ 0.3 mm warp over 100 mm | Verify before final assembly |

Critical interfaces must undergo dry fit testing before torque application.  
Reinforce or shim any printed bracket at high‑torque locations (FEMUR/COXA mounts).  

------------------------------------------------------------
7. FASTENER FITMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

- No force‑fit alignment through bolting.  
- If hole misalignment > 0.5 mm → stop assembly and correct geometry.  
- Fasteners must enter square and seat flat on metal surfaces.  
- Use grade 10.9 hardware for primary loads / A2‑70 for light subsystems.  

------------------------------------------------------------
8. CUMULATIVE ERROR CONTROL
------------------------------------------------------------

To avoid geometric stack‑up error:  
- Do **not** fully torque structure before confirming symmetry.  
- Verify primary ring flatness prior to upper assembly.  
- Confirm vertical alignment before installing cross bracing.  
- Mount printed core brackets only after main ring rigidity is validated.  

Assembly must progress in documented validation stages described in Test & Validation Master Plan Phase 1 – 3.  

------------------------------------------------------------
9. INSPECTION CHECKPOINTS
------------------------------------------------------------

**Checkpoint 1** – Primary ring diameter and flatness verified (V3 target 1143 ± 1 mm).  
**Checkpoint 2** – Vertical posts aligned and cut equal height (1143 ± 0.5 mm).  
**Checkpoint 3** – Upper ring checked for concentricity and level.  
**Checkpoint 4** – Radial braces and printed gussets installed / verified against drawings.  
**Checkpoint 5** – Final torque inspection of all fasteners (verify 8 N·m M6 hardware).  

All measurements logged under /10_Lifecycle/Fabrication_Inspection_Record.txt.  

------------------------------------------------------------
10. DESIGN INTENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Maintain dimensional accuracy and symmetry throughout manufacture and assembly of the OBSIDIAN‑8 V3 frame.  
Ensure all metal and printed components assemble without residual stress or forced fit conditions.  
Preserve rigidity targets for Torsional Stiffness Specification v1 and structural validation testing.  
All fabrication and inspection records become part of the Configuration Management audit package.
