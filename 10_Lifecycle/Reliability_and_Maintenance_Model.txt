1. PURPOSE

Define the Reliability and Maintenance Model (RMM) for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Ensure long-term operational reliability across mechanical, electrical, and software subsystems
Provide structured maintenance schedules and procedures
Support fleet readiness, auditability, and configuration traceability
2. RELIABILITY METRICS
Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF): target 500 hours for mechanical actuators, 1,000 hours for electronics
Mean Time To Repair (MTTR): ≤ 2 hours for replaceable modules
Subsystem Availability: ≥ 99% during operational cycles
Failure Mode Coverage: all high RPN (≥25) FMEA items monitored and mitigated
Redundancy Metrics: critical systems have ≥2 independent fail-safe mechanisms
3. MAINTENANCE CATEGORIES

3.1 Preventive Maintenance:

Scheduled inspections of frame, joints, actuators, and wiring
BMS and energy system diagnostics
Firmware and ROS2 node updates
Cleaning of sensors, cameras, and mechanical joints

3.2 Predictive Maintenance:

Sensor-based monitoring of actuator torque, temperature, and vibration
Battery SOC and cycle tracking
Software logs and telemetry analysis for early fault detection

3.3 Corrective Maintenance:

Fault isolation and repair of failed hardware
Node restart or firmware reflash for software issues
Calibration or replacement of misaligned sensors

3.4 Field Maintenance:

Authorized technicians perform maintenance on-site
Any field modification must follow documented CR and risk assessment
Maintain detailed service and maintenance logs
4. MAINTENANCE PROCEDURES
Daily Pre-Mission Check:
Verify battery charge and energy distribution
Inspect mechanical joints and actuators visually
Confirm ROS2 nodes and watchdog status
Weekly Inspection:
Check frame integrity and alignment
Verify thermal management and cooling systems
Test kill-line and reactive safety overrides
Monthly Calibration:
Sensor suite calibration (IMU, cameras, LiDAR)
Actuator torque and position validation
Energy model SOC verification
Annual Overhaul:
Complete inspection of mechanical, electrical, and control systems
Firmware and software full update and regression testing
Document configuration, RPN, and any updates to baselines
5. LOGGING AND TRACEABILITY
All maintenance events must be logged with:
Date, technician, serial number
Subsystems serviced
Actions taken and parts replaced
Logs stored for ≥5 years to ensure auditability
Any anomaly triggering corrective maintenance must update FMEA and deployment profile records
6. RELIABILITY VALIDATION
Periodic endurance testing to confirm MTBF predictions
Verification of failover systems under controlled failure conditions
Analysis of maintenance logs to detect recurring failure patterns
Continuous update of preventive schedules based on fleet data
7. DESIGN INTENT
Maximize uptime and operational readiness of OBSIDIAN-8 units
Maintain predictable and auditable maintenance cycles
Ensure early fault detection and mitigation to prevent mission-critical failures
Integrate maintenance data into configuration and deployment records
Preserve system integrity, safety, and fleet reliability over full lifecycle
