1. PURPOSE

Define Phase 2 – Industrialization for OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

Transition from prototype development to repeatable industrial production
Standardize mechanical, electrical, software, and assembly processes
Ensure compliance with safety, quality, and deployment standards
Prepare for fleet manufacturing, integration, and validation
2. OBJECTIVES
Implement production-ready design revisions from Phase 1 validation
Establish controlled supply chain and manufacturing processes
Document assembly, calibration, and testing procedures
Validate system reliability, safety, and deployment readiness
Integrate quality control, configuration management, and traceability
3. INDUSTRIALIZATION PROCESS

3.1 Mechanical Systems:

Standardize frame fabrication, actuator installation, and mass/Cg compliance
Validate structural tolerances and repeatability
Establish inspection and quality checkpoints

3.2 Electrical Systems:

Standardize power distribution, BMS integration, and wiring harnesses
Verify component placement, mounting, and strain relief
Test redundancy, thermal management, and watchdog functionality

3.3 Software Systems:

Lock deployment profiles and firmware versions for production
Validate ROS2 nodes, message definitions, and interfaces
Establish automated build, test, and deployment pipelines

3.4 Assembly and Calibration:

Implement step-by-step assembly instructions
Perform sensor calibration, actuator tuning, and energy validation
Ensure configuration records are updated per unit

3.5 Quality Assurance:

Perform acceptance, endurance, movement, docking, and failure injection tests
Log all deviations and corrective actions
Maintain full audit trail of assembly and test data
4. PRODUCTION CONTROL
Configuration Management: enforce baselines for mechanical, electrical, software, and production revisions
Version Control: all units tagged with V[Major].[Minor].[Revision] for hardware, firmware, and documentation
Change Control: no deviation without formal Change Request (CR) approval
Traceability: all units tracked by serial number, calibration version, and deployment profile
5. VALIDATION AND VERIFICATION
Pre-Deployment Testing: ensure all units pass Phase 2 acceptance tests
System-Level Validation: verify integration of mechanical, electrical, software, and swarm subsystems
Audit Readiness: verify all records, logs, and configurations are complete and retrievable
6. SAFETY AND COMPLIANCE
Confirm reactive safety, kill-line, and watchdog functionality operational
Validate compliance with safety hierarchy and cybersecurity rules
Ensure field-deployed units meet Phase 2 industrialization standards
7. DESIGN INTENT
Transition OBSIDIAN-8 from prototype to scalable, industrial-grade production
Ensure repeatable assembly, safe operation, and reliable performance
Provide traceable records for configuration, testing, and audit
Maintain flexibility for deployment profiles while preserving core safety and performance
