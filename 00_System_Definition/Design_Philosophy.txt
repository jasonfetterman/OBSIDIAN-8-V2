1. PURPOSE

Define the guiding principles, architectural priorities, and engineering mindset for the design, development, and deployment of OBSIDIAN-8 V3.

The philosophy ensures:

Safety-first engineering
Predictable system behavior
Traceable and auditable changes
Operational reliability
Modular and upgradeable architecture
Security subordinate to safety but mandatory for deployment
2. DESIGN PRINCIPLES

2.1 Safety Dominance

Hardware and reactive control always take priority over software commands.
Kill-line, watchdog, and reactive safety mechanisms cannot be overridden by network or swarm commands.

2.2 Modularity and Extensibility

Subsystems are loosely coupled with clearly defined interfaces.
Hardware and software modules can be upgraded independently.
Swappable sensors and actuators must not compromise baseline safety.

2.3 Deterministic Control

System responses must be predictable under all expected operational conditions.
Real-time behavior is prioritized over non-critical processing.
Control loops include monitoring and fallback mechanisms.

2.4 Security Integration

Security measures must protect communication, credentials, and firmware integrity.
Cybersecurity controls are always subordinate to reactive safety systems.
Threat modeling and validation guide design decisions.

2.5 Reliability and Redundancy

Critical subsystems include redundancy or fail-safe modes.
Hardware and software are monitored continuously for degradation or faults.
Failures must be contained, predictable, and mitigated.

2.6 Traceability and Auditability

Every change in hardware, software, or calibration is tracked via CM procedures.
All deployed units maintain a complete deployment_profile and configuration record.
Design intent and rationale are documented for each subsystem.

2.7 Human-Centered Interaction

Operator interfaces prioritize clarity, authority transparency, and minimal cognitive load.
Alerts, logs, and notifications are unambiguous and actionable.
3. ENGINEERING MINDSET
Anticipate failure: design proactively to mitigate risks before they occur.
Validate continuously: testing and verification are integral at every stage.
Maintain discipline: enforce CM, FMEA, and cybersecurity protocols consistently.
Iterate responsibly: improvements are introduced via controlled CRs and baselines.
Respect operational context: environment, payload, and mission type guide subsystem priorities.
4. DESIGN INTENT

The OBSIDIAN-8 V3 design philosophy ensures that:

All units evolve predictably and safely.
Operational authority and safety hierarchy are preserved.
Failures are managed with minimal impact.
Software, hardware, and network interactions are traceable and auditable.
Security, while crucial, never compromises core safety mechanisms.
System architecture supports long-term maintainability, modularity, and field upgradeability.
