4. FAILURE MODES AND EFFECTS ANALYSIS (FMEA)

Reference: Failure_Modes_and_Effects_Analysis.txt

Failure ID FM-001 to FM-012 across subsystems: Reactive Controller, Mission Compute, Battery, Actuators, Frame, Watchdog, Communication, Sensors, Power Distribution, Docking, Authority, Energy Model
Includes Failure Mode, Cause, Effect, Severity, Occurrence, Detection, RPN, Mitigation
RPN interpretation: ≥25 → review, 15–24 → acceptable with monitoring, <15 → controlled
Review Policy: before Phase 2 exit, after Class A changes, after critical incidents, annual
Design Intent: structured evaluation, prioritized mitigation, proactive discipline
5. DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

Reference: Design_Philosophy.txt

Guiding principles: Safety-first, modularity, deterministic control, integrated security, reliability, traceability, human-centered interaction
Engineering mindset: anticipate failure, validate continuously, maintain discipline, iterate responsibly, respect operational context
Design Intent: predictable evolution, preserved authority and safety hierarchy, contained failures, traceable software/hardware/network interactions, security subordinate to safety
6. SYSTEM CONTROL AUTHORITY MODEL

Reference: System_Control_Authority_Model.txt

Authority Hierarchy: Hardware-level > Reactive Controller > Mission Compute > Local Operator > Swarm > Automated Failsafe
Control Decision Flow: input → verification → reactive check → hardware kill → execution → logging
Conflict Resolution: Hardware > Software, Reactive > Mission, Local > Remote, Swarm ≤ Local Safety
Authority Validation and Testing: penetration testing, forced conflict simulations, logging/audit review
Design Intent: safety preserved, deterministic, auditable, operator authority clear, swarm enhances mission without violating safety
